Over the past century, climate change has transformed from a distant theory to a pressing global emergency. Driven largely by human activities—like burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial pollution—our planet has warmed at an unprecedented rate. This has led to rising sea levels, extreme weather events, vanishing biodiversity, and threats to food and water security. Why is this bad? Because climate change affects everything we depend on—our health, our homes, our ecosystems, and our economies. It's not just about melting ice caps or endangered species; it's about the world we live in and the future we're handing down to the next generation. The longer we wait, the worse it gets. Every degree of warming pushes us closer to irreversible damage. But the good news? We still have time to make a difference. Acting now—through renewable energy, sustainable choices, and global cooperation—means we can protect our planet and build a more resilient, just future for all. Now isn’t just the right time to act—it’s our last best chance.
